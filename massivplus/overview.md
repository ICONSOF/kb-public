# MASSIV+ — Overview

## What it is

MASSIV+ is a Swedish cross-industry initiative that makes climate data shareable, accessible, and actionable across supply chains. Launched 2022, initiated by Microsoft, Alfa Laval, SKF, Sandvik, IKEA, and Volvo Cars. Now expanded to include industry, academia, financial institutions, and public organisations.

In 2025, MASSIV+ received a significant Vinnova grant to operationalise the initiative as a 3-year project (2025–2028).

## The problem

Over 70% of industrial climate impact sits in Scope 3 — indirect emissions across the value chain. Most companies rely on generic industry averages because there is no standardised way to collect, structure, and share real supplier data. SMEs (the bulk of supply chains) lack the resources and tools to measure and share their emissions. Large companies subject to CSRD depend on this supplier data but can't get it at the quality they need.

**The core insight:** Every company can only directly measure its own Scope 1 and 2. When that data is made traceable and shared, it becomes the building blocks for Scope 3 for the next company in the chain. *My Scope 3 is your Scope 1 and 2.*

## How it works

Three integrated components:

1. **Common standard and methodology** — practical framework for collecting, structuring, calculating, and sharing Scope 1–2 data. Aligned with GHG Protocol, ISO 14067, and ESRS
2. **Federated data infrastructure** — built on IDS/Gaia-X principles. Peer-to-peer data sharing where each company retains sovereignty over its own data
3. **AI-based guidance** — especially for SMEs: low-threshold onboarding, automated data structuring, quality checks, and action recommendations
