# CSRD — Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive

## What it is

The CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) is EU legislation that requires large companies to report standardised sustainability information. It replaces the earlier NFRD (Non-Financial Reporting Directive) and significantly expands the scope, depth, and assurance requirements for sustainability reporting.

CSRD entered into application from financial year 2024 onwards (phased by company size).

## Who must report

Reporting requirements are phased in by company size:

| Phase | Companies | From FY |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Large listed companies already subject to NFRD (~500 employees) | 2024 |
| 2 | All other large EU companies (>250 employees OR >€40M turnover OR >€20M balance sheet) | 2025 |
| 3 | Listed SMEs (with opt-out until 2028) | 2026 |
| 4 | Non-EU companies with significant EU operations (>€150M EU net turnover) | 2028 |

## Reporting standards: ESRS

Reports must follow the **European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS)**, developed by EFRAG. There are two types:

- **Cross-cutting standards** — apply to all companies regardless of sector (ESRS 1 general requirements, ESRS 2 general disclosures)
- **Topical standards** — cover specific ESG topics (climate, biodiversity, workers, business conduct)

For climate: **ESRS E1** is the key standard. See `standards/esrs-e1.md`.

## Double materiality

A distinctive feature of CSRD is **double materiality** — companies must assess both:
- **Impact materiality** — how does the company impact the environment and society?
- **Financial materiality** — how do sustainability issues create financial risks and opportunities for the company?

This assessment determines which disclosures are mandatory for a given company (beyond the always-mandatory basics).

## Value chain scope

CSRD requires companies to report on their **entire value chain** — not just their own operations. This is why large companies under CSRD need Scope 3 data from their suppliers. This is the regulatory driver behind MASSIV+.

## VSME — the SME standard

VSME (Voluntary Sustainability Reporting Standard for SMEs) is a simplified voluntary standard for SMEs not directly subject to CSRD. It provides a compatible but lighter-weight framework that:
- Allows SMEs to respond to customer data requests in a structured way
- Prepares them for potential future mandatory reporting
- Aligns with what large companies need for their own Scope 3 calculations

The Cards platform is built around both ESRS (for large companies) and VSME (for SMEs).
