# GHG Protocol — Scope 1, 2, and 3

## What it is

The GHG Protocol (Greenhouse Gas Protocol) is the most widely used international accounting tool for understanding, quantifying, and managing greenhouse gas emissions. Developed by the World Resources Institute (WRI) and WBCSD, it underpins most corporate and product-level carbon accounting standards — including ESRS E1, ISO 14064, and PCF methodologies.

## The three scopes

### Scope 1 — Direct emissions

Emissions from sources owned or controlled by the organisation:
- Combustion of fuel in company-owned vehicles and equipment
- On-site fuel combustion (boilers, furnaces)
- Process emissions (industrial processes that release GHGs)
- Fugitive emissions (refrigerants, leaks)

These are the emissions a company can directly measure and influence.

### Scope 2 — Purchased energy (indirect)

Emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, heat, steam, or cooling consumed by the organisation. The emissions happen at the power plant, not at the company's site, but the company's consumption drives them.

Two accounting methods:
- **Location-based** — uses average grid emission factors for the region
- **Market-based** — uses emission factors from the company's specific energy contracts (renewable energy certificates, power purchase agreements)

### Scope 3 — Value chain (indirect)

All other indirect emissions in the company's value chain — both upstream (suppliers) and downstream (use of products, end-of-life).

Scope 3 is split into 15 categories, including:
- Purchased goods and services (the biggest category for most manufacturers)
- Capital goods
- Fuel and energy-related activities
- Transportation and distribution
- Business travel and commuting
- Use of sold products
- End-of-life treatment

**Scope 3 is typically 70–90% of a manufacturer's total footprint** — but it's also the hardest to measure because it depends on data from thousands of suppliers.

## The MASSIV+ connection

The core insight of MASSIV+: *my Scope 3 is made up of your Scope 1 and 2.*

If every company in a supply chain shares its Scope 1 and 2 data in a standardised, comparable format, Scope 3 calculations become reliable and auditable — instead of relying on industry averages.

MASSIV+ focuses on enabling this by:
1. Standardising Scope 1–2 data collection and reporting (CCDM data model)
2. Making that data shareable across the supply chain (IDS data space)
3. Providing AI guidance to help SMEs calculate their Scope 1–2 correctly (AP3)

## Product Carbon Footprint (PCF)

A PCF quantifies the greenhouse gas emissions associated with a product across all or part of its life cycle. Governed by ISO 14067 and the WBCSD/PACT framework.

PCF is a common use case in both MASSIV+ and Catena-X. The CCDM data model includes product-level emissions data to support PCF calculations.

## Allocation methods

When a facility or process produces multiple products, emissions must be allocated between them. Common methods:
- **Mass allocation** — by weight of output
- **Economic allocation** — by economic value of output
- **Physical allocation** — by a relevant physical parameter (energy content, volume)
- **System expansion** — credit for avoided emissions

Allocation choices significantly affect the resulting PCF number. MASSIV+ and CCDM should specify which methods are accepted to ensure comparability.
