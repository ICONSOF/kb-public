# ESRS E1 — Climate change

## What it is

ESRS E1 is the European Sustainability Reporting Standard for climate change. It is one of the topical standards under CSRD and covers a company's climate-related impacts, risks, opportunities, and strategies.

## Key disclosure areas

ESRS E1 covers:

1. **Transition plan** — the company's strategy for aligning with the Paris Agreement (1.5°C pathway)
2. **Policies and actions** — what the company is doing to reduce climate impact
3. **Targets** — GHG reduction targets (absolute and intensity)
4. **Energy** — energy consumption, energy mix, renewable energy share
5. **GHG emissions** — Scope 1, 2, and 3, with methodologies
6. **Carbon removals and credits** — any offsetting activity
7. **Internal carbon pricing** — if used
8. **Climate-related financial risks** — physical and transition risks

## GHG emissions disclosures

The emissions disclosures in ESRS E1 require:
- Gross Scope 1 GHG emissions
- Gross Scope 2 GHG emissions (location-based and market-based)
- Gross Scope 3 GHG emissions by category
- Total GHG emissions intensity

The Scope 3 requirement is why large companies need supplier data — and why MASSIV+ exists.

## Relationship to GHG Protocol

ESRS E1 is built on the GHG Protocol. Companies reporting under ESRS E1 must follow GHG Protocol accounting and reporting standards for their emissions disclosures. See `standards/ghg-protocol.md`.

## MASSIV+ relevance

The data that MASSIV+ facilitates sharing — Scope 1 and 2 data from suppliers — feeds directly into the Scope 3 disclosures required by ESRS E1 for large companies.

A company like Volvo Cars needs this data from thousands of suppliers to complete its ESRS E1 Scope 3 disclosure. MASSIV+ makes that data available in a standardised, auditable form.
